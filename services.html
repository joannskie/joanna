Article 2: How Being a Working Student Shapes Personal Growth and Future Success

It is common to romanticise being a working student as the pinnacle of ambition and hard effort. That is true, but it is also a path of self-discovery that imparts knowledge that goes well beyond the classroom or the workplace. Working while I was a student had a significant effect on my personality, perspective on life, and path to success in the future. The value of time management was among the first lessons I learnt as a working student.

I had to learn to be careful about how I used my time because I only had so many hours in a day. Procrastination was no longer a choice, as I soon discovered. Every moment needed to be maximised, whether that meant making a thorough work calendar or giving hours for studying. I have improved my ability to set priorities, divide complicated projects into smaller, more manageable ones, and keep my flexibility in the face of unforeseen requests. Beyond merely finishing tasks and getting paid, the experience has made me a more self-sufficient, disciplined, and sympathetic person.

These qualities will help me both in my academic endeavours and when I start my professional career. Although it can be difficult, being a working student is one of the finest ways to get ready for the future. The future is full of limitless possibilities because of the ability to manage several responsibilities, the fortitude to overcome setbacks, and the professional experience acquired along the road.
